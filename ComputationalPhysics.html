Computational + Experimental Physics Projects:

Computational Physics Projects:

Thomson Problem Matlab numerical solution for the Thomson problem: a computational problem involving the optimal arrangement of electrons on a sphere to minimize electric potential. Calculated the equivalence classes of electrons starting from random positions on the sphere's surface, for configurations up to 60 electrons. Analyzed and visualized the results, identifying and characterizing various equilibrium configurations of the electron system.

Adaptaive Runge-Kutta ODE solver: Runge-Kutta fourth-order integrator in MATLAB to solve differential equations efficiently, with dynamic time step adjustments based on per-step error. Conducted numerical experiments on Simple Harmonic and Van Der Pol Oscillators to verify the effectiveness of the integrator, achieving greater accuracy than in fixed time steps.

Schrodinger Equation Solver with Convergence: MATLAB implementation to simulate the Schrödinger Equation in 1 and 2 dimensions using the Crank-Nicolson Discretization method. Conducted convergence tests to ensure accurate and stable numerical solutions Created simulation surveys of Rectangular Potential Barrier, Potential Well, and Double Slit for boosted gaussian wave packets.

Experimental Physics Projects:

Johnson Noise Experiment and Analysis Experimentally quantified Johnson noise from a resistor through circuit construction and data analysis. Utilized Cross Correlation and a Fourier transform in Python to isolate the Johnson noise from the Shot noise and turn the signal into a Power Spectral Density Graph (PSD) to fit a relation to estimate the Johnson Noise. Finally, Cross-Correlation was used to isolate Johnson Noise from Shot Noise and regression was used to extrapolate a relation between Resistance and Electronic Noise and estimated the Boltzman Constant.
